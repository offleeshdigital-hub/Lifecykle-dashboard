Targeting Strategy & Exclusions

Targeting Approach

  • TOF Broad: 18–65, Australia-wide, no interest targeting — let Meta's algorithm optimise
  • TOF Interest: Layered interests per product vertical as backup if broad underperforms
  • MOF: Custom audiences from pixel events (ViewContent, ATC, IC) + engagement audiences
  • BOF: Purchase-based custom audiences segmented by recency
  • ASC: Broad with existing customer cap at 20% to prioritise new customer acquisition

Exclusion Logic

  • TOF campaigns: Exclude all purchasers (180 days) + website visitors (30 days)
  • MOF campaigns: Exclude purchasers (30 days) to avoid wasting retargeting spend
  • BOF cross-sell: Exclude buyers of the specific product being advertised
  • All campaigns: Exclude employees, existing subscribers (where possible via customer list)
  • Geo exclusions: None — all of Australia included. No state-level restrictions needed.

Operational Management

Daily (15 min)

  • Check delivery status & pacing
  • Flag any CPM spikes or delivery issues
  • Monitor for ad rejections
  • Review ROAS by campaign tier

Weekly (1 hour)

  • Creative performance review — identify winners/losers
  • Kill underperformers (see testing framework)
  • Shift budget to top performers
  • Launch new creative tests
  • Update audience exclusions

Monthly (2 hours)

  • Full account audit — structure, spend, ROAS
  • Creative playbook update
  • Audience saturation check (frequency analysis)
  • Budget reallocation across products
  • Report to stakeholders

Decision Framework — Scale, Hold, or Kill

🟢 SCALE — Increase Budget 20%

Trigger when ALL conditions met for 3+ consecutive days:

  • ROAS ≥ 3.0x
  • CPA ≤ $35
  • CTR ≥ 1.5%
  • Frequency < 2.5
  • Spend has exited learning phase

Action: Increase daily budget by 20% max. Never more than once every 3 days. Monitor for 48hrs post-increase.

🟡 HOLD — Monitor Closely

When performance is acceptable but not scalable:

  • ROAS between 2.0x – 2.9x
  • CPA between $35 – $50
  • CTR between 1.0% – 1.5%
  • Frequency between 2.5 – 3.5

Action: Test new creative variants. Adjust audience if frequency rising. Do not increase budget. Review in 5 days.

🔴 KILL — Pause & Diagnose

Trigger when ANY condition met after learning phase:

  • ROAS < 2.0x for 5+ consecutive days
  • CPA > $60
  • CTR < 0.8%
  • Frequency > 4.0
  • $0 revenue after $150+ spend

Action: Pause ad set immediately. Diagnose: is it creative fatigue, audience saturation, or landing page issue? Reallocate budget to winners.

Scaling Methodology — Maintaining Efficiency at Higher Spend

Horizontal Scaling (Primary)

  • Duplicate winning ad sets with fresh audiences
  • Launch new creative angles on proven audiences
  • Expand to Lookalike audiences (1%, 2%, 5%)
  • Test new product bundles / value packs
  • Layer in ASC campaigns with proven winners

Vertical Scaling (Secondary)

  • Increase budget by max 20% per 3-day period
  • Only on ad sets with 3+ days above 3.0x ROAS
  • Monitor CPA for 48hrs after each increase
  • If CPA rises >20%, pause increase and revert
  • Set campaign-level spend limits as safety net

Creative Testing Framework

A repeatable system for testing, iterating, and scaling creative — not a list of ideas

Weekly Creative Cadence

1
Monday — Brief & Concepting
Review previous week's data. Identify winning angles, formats, and hooks. Brief 4–6 new creatives (mix of static + video) based on learnings.
2
Tuesday–Wednesday — Production
Design team produces assets. Static ads in 1080×1080, 1080×1350, 1080×1920. Video ads in 9:16 and 1:1. All variants follow brand guidelines.
3
Thursday — Launch
Upload new creatives into testing ad sets. Each test gets $20–$30/day budget. One variable isolated per test (hook, visual, CTA, or angle).
4
Friday–Sunday — Data Collection
Let tests run untouched. Minimum 1,000 impressions before evaluating. No changes during this window.
5
Monday — Evaluation & Decision
Apply Kill/Hold/Scale criteria. Winners move to scaling campaigns. Losers are paused. Insights logged in Creative Playbook.

Fatigue Management & Creative Playbook

Fatigue Signals

  • Frequency > 3.0 — audience seeing ads too often
  • CTR declining 20%+ week-over-week
  • CPA rising 25%+ with no external factors
  • Negative comments increasing on ad posts

Response Protocol

  • Swap creative immediately — don't wait for weekly cycle
  • Broaden audience to reduce frequency pressure
  • Rotate ad set off for 7 days, then relaunch with fresh creative
  • Promote next-best performer from testing pipeline

Creative Playbook Documentation

Every test result is logged in a shared Google Sheet with these fields:

  • Date launched / Date killed or scaled
  • Product, Audience Segment, Funnel Stage
  • Creative angle, Hook, Format, Visual style
  • Primary metrics: CTR, CPA, ROAS, Hook Rate
  • Outcome: Kill / Iterate / Scale
  • Key Insight (one sentence takeaway)
  • Screenshot of the creative for reference

This playbook becomes the strategic asset. Over time, it reveals exactly which angles, hooks, and formats resonate for each product and audience — eliminating guesswork.
